Meet Your Guides

Miigizi and Winona will travel with you through every lesson, game, and story.

🦅
Miigizi
He/him  ·  Anishinaabe/Ojibwe  ·  Age 8

Miigizi (MEE-gi-zi) means "Eagle" in Ojibwemowin. He lives in Minneapolis with his family, who comes from the White Earth Nation. Every summer he visits his Nokomis (grandmother) to harvest wild rice. He loves basketball, beadwork, and learning the names of birds.

🌻
Winona
She/her  ·  Dakota  ·  Age 8

Winona is a Dakota name meaning "firstborn daughter." Her family is from the Shakopee Mdewakanton Sioux Community but she goes to school in the Twin Cities. Her dad works for the tribe's environmental department. She is curious, loves asking hard questions, and knows the names of plants in Dakota.

⚠ Community Review Note: The names, nation affiliations, and family details for these characters are working placeholders. All character details — including names — should be reviewed and approved by Native community members, cultural liaisons, or tribal education staff before being used with students. Contact your district's Native American parent liaison or tribal education department.

Six Units of Study

🌾
UNIT 1  ·  PILOT

Manoomin / Wild Rice:
A Living Relationship

Miigizi and Winona discover why wild rice is sacred to Anishinaabe peoples and important to Dakota communities — and how tribal nations are protecting it today.

UNIT 2

Dakota Homeland:
Mni Sota Makoce

Explore the Dakota homeland — the land of sky-tinted waters — and meet the vibrant communities who continue to call Minnesota home today.

Social Studies ELA

UNIT 3

Sovereignty &
Self-Governance

What does it mean for a tribal nation to govern itself? Students explore how Minnesota's 11 nations make decisions, run programs, and protect their communities.

Social Studies ELA
🍂
UNIT 4

Seasonal Life &
Land Stewardship

Follow Miigizi and Winona through the four seasons, learning traditional ecological knowledge and how the Fond du Lac Band cares for the land today.

Science Arts ELA
🗣️
UNIT 5

Language Keepers:
Ojibwemowin & Dakota Iapi

Languages carry culture. Meet the people bringing Ojibwemowin and Dakota Iapi back to life — and practice some words yourself using MIIN and Dakhota.org resources.

ELA Arts Social Studies
🗺️
UNIT 6

Treaties &
Place Names

Minnesota's place names tell a story. Students map Dakota and Ojibwe place names, explore what treaties meant, and discover names that are still used today.
