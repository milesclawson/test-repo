Unit 1 · Executive Functioning

The mental skills behind every good decision — attention, working memory, flexible thinking, and self-control.

4 Lessons

Executive function is the air traffic control system of the brain. Students learn what it is, why it peaks in the mid-20s, and — most importantly — how to build it right now through habits, systems, and practice. Lessons cover prioritization, focus, managing distraction, and developing grit.

Monday Check-In

Start every week by looking back — missing work, test make-ups, notes review, and setting a weekly goal.

Repeatable · 18 Weeks

📝 Missing Work Tracker

For each class: list any assignment that is missing, its due date, and your plan to complete it. Nothing leaves Monday without a make-up commitment.

📊 Test & Quiz Review

List any tests from last week, your score, and what you would study differently. If a make-up is needed, write the date it's scheduled.

📓 Notes Review

For each class: most important idea from last week's notes. What are you still confused about? Share one with a partner.

🏆 Weekly Win + Goal

Name one win from last week (any size). Set one specific, measurable goal for this week. Write it as an "I will..." statement.

Teacher note: The Monday Check-In workbook pages (18 weeks, print-ready PDF) are available in Teacher Resources. The matching PPTX slide deck guides the class through each section with a navy blue theme and 3-slide sequence: overview → missing work/test tracker → notes review and goal.

📅

Friday Planning

End every week by looking ahead — deadlines, activities, a full schedule grid, and a commitment to show up ready.

Repeatable · 18 Weeks

📌 Upcoming Deadlines

List every assignment, test, or project due in the next two weeks — class, task, and due date. Highlight anything due Monday or Tuesday.

⚽ Activities Tracker

Mon–Sun: what do you have in the afternoon? Evening? Sports, jobs, family, appointments. See the full picture before you commit to anything.

🗓 Week-at-a-Glance Grid

Monday–Friday with 3PM–10PM time blocks. Fill in fixed commitments first, then schedule study time around them. No blank evenings.

✅ Weekend Commitment

Five specific things you commit to doing this weekend. Not vague goals — actual tasks with a day attached. Sign it. Hold yourself to it.

Teacher note: Friday Planning workbook pages (18 weeks, print-ready PDF) available in Teacher Resources. The matching PPTX slides use a forest green theme and guide the class through 3 slides: overview → deadlines/activities → week grid and commitment.

1–2 page non-fiction with source citation and 5 comprehension questions. Referenced audio file on the reading page.

Pre-printed cue questions in the left column + blank note space. Summary section at bottom of each page.
