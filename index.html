Unit 1 · Basic Life Skills

The essentials every teenager needs — laundry, basic sewing, dishes, and keeping a home organized.

4 Lessons

Students learn the household skills their parents wish they'd been taught earlier. From reading a laundry label to sewing on a button, this unit builds independence and confidence at home.

Unit 2 · Basic Gardening

From soil science to harvest day — students plan, plant, and tend a garden bed across 8 lessons.

8 Lessons

Students learn where food actually comes from and how to grow it. The unit follows the full gardening lifecycle: planning the bed, building healthy soil, composting, planting, managing pests, watering efficiently, and harvesting at the right time. The unit culminates in building a real or simulated garden bed.

#Lesson titleKey skillsFeatures
2.1Garden PlanningSite selection, layout, what to growSimulationStoryReadingVideo
2.2Soil ScienceSoil types, pH, amendments, nutrientsStoryReadingVideo
2.3CompostingBrowns vs. greens, bin setup, troubleshootingStoryReadingVideo
2.4Planting Seeds & StartsSeed depth, spacing, transplant shockStoryReadingVideo
2.5Pests & Plant HealthIdentify pests, organic solutions, companion plantingStoryReadingVideo
2.6Watering & IrrigationWatering schedules, deep vs. shallow, drought signsStoryReadingVideo
2.7HarvestingWhen to pick, how to pick, storing produceStoryReadingVideo
2.8Build a Garden BedRaised bed construction, first planting dayStoryReadingVideo

Unit 3 · Personal Finance & Time Management

Money skills and job-readiness — from budgeting a first paycheck to acing a job interview.

8 Lessons

Students learn how money works before they earn their first paycheck. The unit covers how marketers manipulate spending, the difference between needs and wants, building a real budget, saving strategies, time management, resume writing, job searching, and interview skills.

3.5Time ManagementPriority matrix, scheduling, avoiding procrastinationStoryReadingVideo
3.6Writing a ResumeFormat, action verbs, what to include at 14–15StoryReadingVideo
3.7Job Search SkillsWhere to look, applications, referencesStoryReadingVideo

Unit 4 · Basic Home Repair

Tools, safety, and the confidence to fix things around the house — no calling a landlord required.

5 Lessons

Students learn to identify and safely use common tools, patch walls, fix a leaky faucet, replace a light switch, and maintain a bike. Each lesson emphasizes safety protocols before touching any tool.

#Lesson titleKey skillsFeatures

4.4Basic Electrical (Safe)Replace outlets/switches, circuit breaker, never touch live wiresStoryReadingVideo
4.5Bike Repair & MaintenanceFix a flat, adjust brakes, chain lubricationStoryReadingVideo

From running a babysitting business to CPR — everything needed to be a safe, trusted, and hired babysitter.

Unit 6 · Basic Food Prep

From food safety to the air fryer — students learn to cook real food safely before they leave middle school.

7 Lessons

Students learn the foundation of cooking: food safety and the danger zone, how to use a knife without cutting themselves, cooking vocabulary, and how to operate the appliances they'll actually find at home — air fryer, Instant Pot, convection toaster oven, and no-bake techniques.

#Lesson titleKey skillsFeatures
6.1Food SafetyDanger zone, cross-contamination, the 2-hour ruleSimulationStoryReadingVideo
6.2Knife Safety & SkillsGrip, curl, safe cutting techniques, knife careStoryReadingVideo
6.3Cooking Terms & MethodsSauté, simmer, roast, fold, deglaze — reading a recipeStoryReadingVideo
6.4Air Fryer CookingHow it works, temperature, timing, cleanupStoryReadingVideo
6.5Instant Pot BasicsPressure cooking, steam release, common recipesStoryReadingVideo
6.6Convection & Toaster OvenFan cooking, temp adjustment, broil vs. bakeStoryReadingVideo
6.7No-Bake CookingEnergy balls, overnight oats, parfaits, smoothiesStoryReadingVideo

Each lesson includes

🎧 Audio (EN + ES)

Story and reading narrated in English and Spanish for ELL and accessibility support.

📖 Discussion story

Realistic fiction featuring 4 recurring Friendly Hills students — Maya, Jordan, Sofia, and Cam.

📰 Informational reading

1–2 page non-fiction reading with 5 comprehension questions per lesson.

📺 YouTube video

Curated video with watch-for prompt and an alternate backup video per lesson.

✏️ Cornell notes

Pre-filled cues + blank note section in the student workbook. Promotes active note-taking.

🚪 Exit ticket

One targeted question per lesson to check understanding before dismissal.
